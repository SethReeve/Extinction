The Japanese Sea Lion

Its Life

The Japanese Sea Lion lived in the Sea of Japan. It was considered a subspecies of the California Sea Lion but became its own species in 2003.

The Japanese Sea Lions mainly breed on open sandy beaches. The males were a dark grey with lengths up to 2.5 meters. The females were a lighter colour and had lengths up to 1.64 meters.

Human Uses and Extinction

The Japanese Sea Lion was terrible to eat. The fisher-men ended up killing them and using them for blubber. They did this mainly to get the Sea Lions out of the way to fish for better food.

It was considered to be entinct in 1970. its extinction was helped by the Japanese commercial harvest that ended in the 1940s.
